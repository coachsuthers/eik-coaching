W1
Shape & Compactness
Establishing defensive structure
90 min
Warm-Up Rondo Maze 15 min
Possession Rondo with Transition · 5v2 × 2 Grids · 14 Players
Blue (keep):retain possession under pressure — replicate keeping the ball to relieve defensive pressure
Red (win):intercept or tackle, then drive/pass the ball through the 4m cone goal — replicate defending as a pair and exploiting transition
Two simultaneous 5v2 rondos — all 14 players active across two 14m × 14m grids (5 blue + 2 red per grid; GK plays outfield in Grid 2). A 4m cone goal sits behind each blue team's end. When red wins the ball: the 2 defenders immediately try to dribble or pass through the cone goal. The 3 blue players nearest the goal must sprint back to defend it — the other 2 apply immediate pressure on the ball. This replicates transitioning from attack to defence, organising a compact defensive unit, and protecting space behind you. Rotate red defenders every 3 minutes.
Two 14m × 14m grids — 7 players per grid
GK plays outfield (Grid 2, blue team)
4m cone goal at each grid's defending end
W1 Warm-Up — Rondo Goal Game (5v2 × 2 Grids)
Intro Mid-Block Gate Game 30 min
Functional Practice — Conditioned 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):block all central lanes; force attackers wide; earn points for channelling play through wide gates — replicating a compact defensive mid-block
Attackers (6):find and exploit gaps in the central corridor; score through central gates or on goal — replicating penetrating a defensive block
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK on half pitch. Central corridor (4m wide) marked by cones/poles. Defending team earns 1pt for forcing play through a wide gate and 2pts if they win possession in the wide channel. Attacking team score by passing through 3 central gates or shooting on the main goal. Coaches rotate players every 10 minutes to ensure everyone experiences both roles.
3 central corridor gates, 2 wide gates
GK on full goal — 6 outfield attackers vs 7 defenders
W1 Intro — Mid-Block Gate Game
Main Conditioned Game 35 min
Small-Sided Conditioned Game — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):maintain compact shape; block central third; score only by breaking out through a wide counter-attack channel — replicating winning the ball and quickly exploiting width
Attackers (6):build through the central third with possession; earn bonus points for 5+ consecutive passes in the central zone — replicating structured attacking play against a block
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK on the full half pitch. The defending team can only score by winning the ball and playing out through one of two wide counter-attack channels (coned at touchline). The attacking team earn 2 bonus points for completing 5 consecutive passes in the central zone. Coach applies a brief freeze (max 3 times per game) to correct compact shape — play restarts within 20 seconds.
6v7+GK — full width half pitch
Wide counter-attack channels coned on both touchlines

W2
Press Triggers & Cues
When & how to engage
90 min
Warm-Up Trigger Relay Tag 15 min
Activation Drill — 7v7 (14 Players)
Dribblers (7 inc. GK):retain possession of their ball and evade defenders — replicating an attacker keeping the ball under pressure and reading space
Defenders (7):shadow and jockey passively until "GO!" — then engage instantly and aggressively — replicating reading a press trigger and committing collectively
All 14 players in a 30m × 20m area split into two groups of 7 (bibs). One group dribbles freely with a ball each; the other group shadows their paired opponent without engaging. On the coach's trigger cue ("GO!"), defenders must transition instantly from passive jockey to full aggressive press. The dribbler tries to escape; the defender tries to win the ball. Rotate groups every 90 seconds — nobody stands outside.
7 dribblers (inc. GK) + 7 defenders — all paired
7 balls active; bibs distinguish groups
W2 Warm-Up — Trigger Relay Tag
Intro Press or Hold — Decision Boxes 30 min
Functional Practice — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):read body shape of ball-carrier and decide whether to press or hold; when ball enters wide zone all ball-side defenders squeeze — replicating a coordinated press triggered by opponent's poor body shape
Attackers (6):use body shape (back to goal, turns) to invite pressure then spin away; find the wide zone and create 2v1s — replicating using body position to manipulate a press
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK across the half pitch divided into 3 horizontal zones. Attackers start in Zone 1 and try to progress forward. When an attacker has their back to goal — the press trigger — the nearest defender must immediately engage. When an attacker faces forward (goal-side), defenders hold their shape and jockey. When the ball enters either wide zone, all ball-side defenders squeeze in 3+ to trap. Coach gives live verbal feedback; rotate roles every 10 minutes.
Three clearly coned horizontal zones across half pitch
GK active in defensive unit — 6v7+GK
W2 Intro — Press or Hold Decision Boxes
Main High Press SSG 35 min
Small-Sided Conditioned Game — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):initiate collective press within 3 seconds of turnover; score on 2 mini-goals by quick counter-press recovery — replicating winning the ball back immediately in dangerous areas
Attackers (6):retain the ball and build forward; earn a free shot if defenders fail to press in 3 seconds — replicating exploiting a disorganised defensive team
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK on the full half pitch. The defending team must initiate a collective press within 3 seconds of losing the ball — failure grants the attacking team a free shot at goal. The defending team can score by winning the ball and playing quickly into one of 2 mini-goals on the halfway line. Verbal press triggers between players are mandatory; silent pressing earns the opposition a possession reset. Coach enforces the 3-second rule strictly.
Full goal + GK; 2 mini-goals on halfway line
6v7+GK; 3-second collective press rule enforced
W2 Main — High Press SSG
Key Coaching Points
Identify trigger: back to goal, heavy touch, backwards pass = press
Press as a unit — first to ball sets direction, others cover
Verbal communication is non-negotiable
W3
Channel Defence
Trapping ball in wide areas
90 min
Warm-Up Overload Channel Game 15 min
Directional Possession Game — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Attackers (6):start with the ball in one wide channel; pass or carry the ball across the central zone to a teammate waiting in the opposite wide channel — replicating switching play to find space
Defenders (7+GK):prevent the ball reaching the far wide channel; when the ball is in a channel, 3 nearest defenders must close in immediately and trap — replicating channelling and overloading the ball-side
Pitch divided into three vertical zones: two wide channels (8m wide) either side and a central zone between them. 6 attackers start with the ball in the left channel; their aim is to pass or dribble across the central zone and successfully play the ball into the right channel — scoring 1pt for each successful switch. 7 defenders + GK must prevent this; when the ball enters a channel, the 3 nearest defenders must immediately close down and trap the ball-carrier against the boundary. The GK plays as an outfield defender. If defenders win the ball they try to keep possession for 5 passes before it restarts. Ball starts with attackers and restarts with attackers after each point or turnover. Rotate who holds the ball-side every 5 minutes.
Pitch split into 3 vertical zones (8m wide channels + central zone)
GK plays as outfield defender — 6v7+GK total
Ball starts with attackers in left channel; aim to reach right channel
W3 Warm-Up — Wide Channel Switch Game (6v7+GK)
Intro Touchline Trap Drill 30 min
Functional Practice — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):angle body to show wide attacker towards the touchline; first defender pins the attacker, second cuts off the pass back inside — replicating using the touchline as an extra defender
Attackers (6):receive in wide areas and create options — attempt a cutback cross into the box, a pass inside, or drive forward — replicating a wide attacker under defensive pressure
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK across the full half pitch. The coach or a waiting player feeds the ball into a wide attacker. The nearest defender immediately shows them towards the touchline; a second defender closes off the cutback lane. Attackers score by completing a successful cutback cross to a runner in the box. Defenders earn 1pt for winning possession in the wide channel and 2pts for forcing a throw-in or ball out of play. Waiting player feeds a second ball in immediately to keep tempo high; rotate players every 4 minutes.
Full half pitch; touchline used as extra defender
6v7+GK; second ball feeds in immediately on out of play
W3 Intro — Touchline Trap Drill
Main Wide Zone Lockdown Game 35 min
Small-Sided Conditioned Game — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):shepherd attackers into wide trap zones; earn 2pts for winning possession in the wide channel and 1pt for forcing a throw-in or corner — replicating a structured wide-press and turning defence into attack
Attackers (6):retain possession and build through the central zone; earn 2pts for completing 5 consecutive passes in the central zone — replicating breaking a compact defensive block with patient possession
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK on the full half pitch. Pitch divided into three vertical zones: two wide trap zones (marked by cones) and a central zone. Defending team earns 2pts for winning possession within the wide channel through a tackle or interception, and 1pt for forcing a throw-in or corner in the wide zone. Attacking team earn 2pts if they retain possession through the central zone with 5+ consecutive passes. GK can organise the touchline trap vocally throughout. Coach uses freeze (max twice per game) to correct trap shape.
Cones marking central and wide trap zones
6v7+GK; GK organises wide trap vocally
W3 Main — Wide Zone Lockdown
Key Coaching Points
Angle of first defender forces winger to the touchline, not inside
Second defender arrives quickly — cut off the back pass
Touchline is your friend — reduce attacker's options to one
W4
Defensive Transitions
Winning it back quickly
90 min
Warm-Up Box-to-Box Transition Rondo 15 min
Transition Rondo — 5v2 × 2 Boxes (14 Players)
Possession team (5):retain the ball from the pressing 2 — replicating keeping possession to maintain defensive shape and frustrate the press
Pressing pair (2):win the ball then sprint it through the corridor into the opposite box — replicating transitioning instantly from defending to attacking after a turnover
Two 15m × 15m boxes connected by a 6m wide corridor. 14 players split evenly: 5 blue + 2 red per box running simultaneous 5v2 rondos. When red wins the ball they immediately sprint or pass the ball through the corridor into the opposite box and join play there as attackers. The 5 blue in the far box must now defend against the arriving pair (now 5v2 again in that box). Continuous and seamless — no resets. Coach times transitions; target is under 4 seconds from win to far box entry. GK plays outfield in one box.
Two 15m × 15m boxes connected by 6m corridor — all 14 active
GK plays outfield; coach times transitions — target under 4 secs
W4 Warm-Up — Box-to-Box Transition Rondo
Intro Counter-Press Circuit 30 min
Counter-Press Circuit — 4v4+N × 3 Grids (14 Players)
Team in possession:keep the ball away from the pressing team, using the neutral to create overloads — replicating building possession while under a defensive press
Team out of possession:immediately counter-press on every turnover — neutral joins the pressing team to create a 5v4 overload — replicating the collective reaction when losing the ball in a real game
Three simultaneous 4v4+1 neutral grids (4+4+4+1 neutral = 13 outfield + GK outfield = 14). In each grid, one team has possession with the neutral player always joining them (5v4). On every transition of possession the team that just lost the ball must immediately counter-press — any player who walks or jogs during the counter-press earns their team a minus point. Neutral player always switches to join the new possession team. Rotate who plays neutral every 5 minutes. No resets between possessions; play is continuous.
Three 18m × 18m grids — all 14 active (GK plays outfield)
Neutral (N) always joins team in possession; rotate neutral every 5 mins
W4 Intro — Counter-Press Circuit
Main Transition & Reset Game 35 min
Small-Sided Conditioned Game — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):get compact and goal-side within 5 seconds of losing possession; earn 2pts for winning the ball and playing out through a wide counter-attack channel — replicating defensive organisation after losing the ball
Attackers (6):build possession and exploit space before defenders reset; if defence fail 5-second rule earn a free 3v1 on goal — replicating exploiting a disorganised defence during transition
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK on the full half pitch. Within 5 seconds of losing possession, all defending players must be goal-side and in a compact shape — failure results in the attacking team receiving a free 3v1 on goal. Defending team earn 2pts for winning the ball and playing it out through a wide counter-attack channel. Coach or assistant head coach times the 5-second reset strictly. Rolling substitutions keep all 14 players involved with no player sitting out for more than 5 minutes.
Full goal + GK; wide counter-attack channels coned on touchlines
6v7+GK; 5-second compact reset rule strictly enforced
W4 Main — Transition & Reset (6v7+GK)
Key Coaching Points
Transition is the most dangerous moment — react immediately, collectively
Get compact first, then press — don't chase individually
Ball-side press; far-side narrows and covers
W5
Defending Set Pieces
Wide delivery & box organisation
90 min
Warm-Up Aerial Duel Warm-Up Circuit 15 min
Technical Activation — 7 Pairs (14 Players)
Defender:win the aerial duel cleanly, using correct footwork and body position to outcompete the feeder — replicating defending aerial deliveries in and around the box
Feeder:serve an accurate aerial ball to challenge the defender; challenge competitively after 2 rounds — replicating an attacking player contesting an aerial duel
14 players in 7 pairs spread at cones around the penalty area perimeter. One player serves an aerial ball (throw or lofted kick), the other defends it — winning the ball cleanly, assessing keeper vs defender responsibility, and managing body shape. After each duel the pair swaps roles. Three clockwise rotations so every pair defends from a different area. GK pairs with one player and coaches that pair on positioning and communication while also serving deliveries. All 7 pairs work simultaneously — no queuing.
7 pairs around penalty area — all 14 active simultaneously
GK coaches 1 pair and serves aerial balls; 3 clockwise rotations
W5 Warm-Up — Aerial Duel Circuit
Intro Wide Cross Defence Organisation 30 min
Functional Practice — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):hold compact zonal shape in and around the box; GK communicates who takes the cross ("keeper!" or "away!"); defend the delivery then immediately play out wide — replicating organised box defence against wide deliveries
Attackers (6):deliver crosses from pre-set wide positions and attack the box with late runs — replicating overloading the box from wide areas against a zonal defensive structure
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK. Coach or attacker feeds ball into one of two pre-set wide positions. The wide attacker delivers a cross into the box; the defensive unit of 7 + GK holds a compact zonal shape, defending the delivery together. GK commands clearly: "keeper!" for balls they will claim, "away!" for clearances. After each delivery the defending unit immediately counter-attacks by playing through a wide channel (simulating winning the ball and transitioning). After each delivery rotate the wide deliverer. Rotate full groups every 4 minutes — 4 min on, 4 min rest, 4 min back on.
6v7+GK; crosses from pre-set left and right wide positions
GK communicates throughout; 4 min on/off rotation
W5 Intro — Wide Cross Defence
Main Attack v Defence Live Game 35 min
Small-Sided Conditioned Game — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defenders (7+GK):hold compact box shape; GK communicates; clear the delivery and immediately play out via a wide channel to earn points — replicating defending wide deliveries and transitioning immediately after a clearance
Attackers (6):must touch the ball in a wide zone before crossing or shooting — no direct central shots allowed; score by converting from a cross — replicating building wide to overload the box against a structured defence
6 attackers vs 7 defenders + GK on the full half pitch. The attacking team must touch the ball in a wide channel before they can cross or shoot — no direct central efforts allowed. Defending team earn 1pt for clearing the ball from a cross situation and 2pts if they then successfully play out through a wide counter-attack channel. A second ball is kept ready for immediate restart after out-of-play situations to maintain tempo. Rolling 5-minute rotations ensure all 14 players are involved in both roles across the session.
6v7+GK — mandatory wide touch before all crosses/shots
Second ball ready; 5-min rolling rotation for all 14 players
W5 Main — Attack v Defence Live
Key Coaching Points
Stay compact — don't be drawn out until cross is struck
GK communicates "keeper!" or "away!" — clear commands remove hesitation
Win the second ball — clear, then press the wide player

Full Integration Game
Applying the complete system

Warm-Up Programme Review Rondo 15 min
Possession Rondo with Transition — 5v2 × 2 Grids (14 Players)
Possession team (5):retain the ball, apply press triggers, stay compact — replicating all concepts from the 6-week programme at once
Pressing pair (2):read the press trigger and win the ball; score through the cone goal on transition — applying all defensive principles independently without coach instruction
Return to the Week 1 5v2 rondo goal game format (two grids, 7 players per grid, cone goals). Players must now simultaneously apply: compact shape (W1), press triggers (W2), touchline trap awareness (W3), and immediate transition (W4). This is player-led — players call their own rotations and name the coaching point they are applying out loud as they do it. Coaches observe only, offering positive reinforcement. W6 Warm-Up — Programme Review Rondo
Intro Combination Scenario Drill 30 min
Multi-Phase Functional Practice — All 14 Players
Defending team:apply all programme themes in sequence — press trigger recognition → mid-block shape → channel trap — replicating a complete 90-minute defensive game plan condensed into 8 minutes
Attacking team:test defensive shape with varied methods — close-range possession, central penetration, then wide attack — replicating a full attacking game plan against a structured defence
All 14 players in 3 rapid-fire linked phases triggered by coach whistle. Phase ①: 4v4 press trigger rondo (each grid, 8 players); Phase ②: on whistle, teams expand immediately to 6v7+GK mid-block gate game across the full half pitch; Phase ③: on second whistle, one team attacks via wide channel, defending team executes a touchline channel trap. Each full cycle lasts 8 minutes; all transitions between phases must happen within 10 seconds. Three full cycles with 2-minute swap. GK rotates role between phases. No coaching during cycles — players self-organise.
All 14 active — phases triggered by whistle, transitions within 10 secs

Conditioned Match — 6v7+GK (14 Players)
Defending team (7+GK):apply all 4 programme themes independently — compact shape, press triggers, channel trap, 5-second transition reset — replicating a full competitive match using the complete defensive system
Attacking team (6):attack with intent and variety; test every aspect of the defensive system — replicating a realistic opposition who will probe wide, central, and in transition
